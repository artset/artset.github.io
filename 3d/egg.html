that egg energy

May 2019, Design Studio

Plywood, acrylic paint.

As a joke, I had made a bunch of egg furniture prototypes, from cracked-egg chairs to egg yolk stools. This series of explorations led to an abstracted egg table made of 1/4 inch plywood. I had a moment of revelation by notioning the essence of the egg through negative space by using a lively semicircle to indicate the yolk. Through this process, I learned how to use a ton of different kinds of saws, used a drill for the first time in my life, and created a pretty solid table.

Here are some other designs that I had played with. I took inspiration from Noguchi and playful mid century modern furniture. This has sparked my interest for furniture design and I hope to do more woodworking in the future.
