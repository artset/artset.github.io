that egg energy

May 2019, Design Studio

Plywood, wood glue, screws, acrylic paint.

As a joke, I had made a bunch of egg furniture prototypes, from cracked-egg chairs to egg yolk stools. This series of explorations led to an abstracted egg table made of 1/4 inch plywood. I wanted to indicate the the egg through the negative space by using lively curves through the space. Through this process, I learned how to use a ton of different kinds of saws, used a drill for the first time in my life, and created a stable table. For the future, I hope to find a gorgeous plant and let it grow through the egg yolk.

Here are some other designs that I had played with. I took inspiration from noguchi and the playful mid century modern furniture in Asterios Polyp!
