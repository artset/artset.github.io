During summer 2018, I helped Andrew Duca and George Lee transform their basic wireframes into high fidelity prototypes to help them build their app. In addition, I was involved in iterating upon their wireframes to improve the UX and design complementary graphics to easily explain how to use the app.

The purpose of Gaggle is to crowd source purchasing of groceries and food. For research, I reviewed and noted the design decisions in similar apps such as Postmates, Uber eats, and Lyft. A crucial difference b/w those apps and Gaggle is that Gaggle aims to seamlessly combine the delivery and shopping into a single app.
View the prototype.
In the beginning, here were some preliminary styles I had designed.

Based on feedback to communicate the approachable, reliable, and connection-oriented nature of the app, I iterated to create the final design styles, using Futura PT for the heading and Proxima Nova for the body.
Afterwards, I split the navigation functions, designing logical tracks for the deliver and shopper pages. Based on the refined wireframe plan, I constructed the rest of the pages and iterated upon them for clarity and convenience. I added a popular items page per store, and a quick filter search design. I used feedback from potential users of the app to refine the design.

Here are the iterations for the graphics explanations and the final!

Through this project, I refined my knowledge of adobe XD, learned illustrator, and used iterative design to create the best UI for this app. In the future, I want to be able to have the opportunity for user testing in order to refine the functionality of my design.
