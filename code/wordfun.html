light/weight

Jan 2019 - May 2019,

Python, d3.js, Javascript, InDesign, Illustrator.

I created a collection of experimentation projects that involve literature, visualization, and code. There is a mix of web apps, such as a poetry generator and a fridge magnet space, and more physical works, such as zines. See the entire collection. Here, I will talk about light/weight, my most recent project.

Ft. Kurt Vonnegut's Sirens of Titan, translated into a bar graph.

For this project, I was contemplating the rhythm of sentences â€” long sentences with polysyndetons and commas galore feel fluid, yet short sentences are are abrupt. These long sentences, paradoxically, are light because of their fluidity. In contrast, short sentences are often used to portray heaviness. From this, I decided to do visualizations by translating weight into color. I wrote a python script that used parameters such as sentence length, word length, stop word count, and adjective count to map to a number between 0 and 255 to translate to a color on the greyscale. Then, I transferred the data into json files to be automated by d3 into visualizations. Lastly, I put on my love of brutalism and graphic design to create the zine, translating these visualizations into a celebration of the text. I printed the zine and binded it myself.
